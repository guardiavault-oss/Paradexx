# Privacy Policy

**Last Updated: January 2024**

## 1. Introduction

GuardiaVault ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our platform for digital inheritance management.

## 2. Information We Collect

### 2.1 Account Information
- Email address
- Password (hashed, never stored in plaintext)
- Wallet address (if connected)
- Full name (optional, for death verification)

### 2.2 Vault Information
- Vault names and configurations
- Check-in intervals and preferences
- Guardian and beneficiary contact information
- Encrypted metadata (we cannot decrypt this data)

### 2.3 Technical Information
- IP address
- Browser type and version
- Device information
- Usage analytics (anonymized)

### 2.4 Death Verification Data (Optional)
- SSN hash (SHA-256, one-way hash only)
- Date of birth
- Last known location
- Death certificate verification status

## 3. How We Use Your Information

### 3.1 Service Provision
- Create and manage your vaults
- Send check-in reminders
- Facilitate guardian and beneficiary communications
- Process death verification requests

### 3.2 Communication
- Send important service updates
- Respond to support requests
- Send guardian and beneficiary invitations

### 3.3 Security
- Detect and prevent fraud
- Verify identity
- Enforce terms of service

### 3.4 Legal Compliance
- Comply with applicable laws
- Respond to legal requests
- Protect our rights and safety

## 4. Data Security

### 4.1 Encryption
- **Client-Side Encryption**: All sensitive data (recovery phrases, seed phrases) is encrypted on your device before transmission
- **Transit Encryption**: All data transmitted via TLS 1.3
- **Storage**: Encrypted database with access controls

### 4.2 Zero-Knowledge Architecture
- **We Never Store**: Private keys, recovery phrases, or seed phrases in plaintext
- **Shamir Secret Sharing**: Fragments are encrypted and stored separately
- **No Access**: Our platform cannot decrypt your vault contents

### 4.3 Security Measures
- Regular security audits
- Access controls and authentication
- Monitoring for suspicious activity
- Data backup and recovery procedures

## 5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

### 5.1 We Do NOT Share
- Your private keys
- Your recovery phrases
- Encrypted vault contents
- SSN or other sensitive identification (beyond necessary verification)

### 5.2 We May Share
- **Guardians**: Contact information to facilitate vault management (with your permission)
- **Beneficiaries**: Contact information when vault is triggered (as per your instructions)
- **Service Providers**: Trusted third parties for email delivery, payment processing (Stripe)
- **Legal Requirements**: When required by law or legal process

### 5.3 Third-Party Services
- **Stripe**: Payment processing (see Stripe's privacy policy)
- **SendGrid**: Email delivery
- **Twilio**: SMS notifications
- **Sentry**: Error tracking (anonymized)

### 6.2 Correction

### 6.3 Deletion

## 7. Retention

### 7.1 Active Accounts
- Data retained while account is active
- Minimum retention for legal compliance: 7 years

### 7.2 Deleted Accounts
- Immediate deletion of personal information
- Encrypted vault data may be retained for 30 days for recovery purposes
- After 30 days, all data is permanently deleted

### 7.3 Triggered Vaults
- Data retained for beneficiary access
- Retention period: As specified in vault configuration
