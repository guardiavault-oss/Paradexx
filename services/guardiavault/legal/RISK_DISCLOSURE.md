## 1. Cryptocurrency Asset Risks

### 1.1 Market Volatility
- Cryptocurrency prices are extremely volatile
- Asset values can drop 50% or more in short periods
- Your heirs may receive assets worth significantly less than at vault creation
- Past performance does not predict future performance

### 1.2 Liquidity Risks
- Some cryptocurrencies may have limited liquidity
- Beneficiaries may struggle to sell assets at fair market value
- Market manipulation may affect prices
- Regulatory actions may impact liquidity

### 1.3 Regulatory Risks
- Cryptocurrency regulations are evolving globally
- Bans or restrictions may prevent access to assets
- Tax treatment may change
- Beneficiaries may face unexpected tax liabilities

## 2. Technology Risks

### 2.1 Smart Contract Risks
- **Bugs**: Despite audits, smart contracts may contain bugs
- **Exploits**: Malicious actors may exploit vulnerabilities
- **Irreversibility**: Smart contract errors cannot be reversed
- **Loss of Funds**: Bugs may result in permanent loss of assets

### 2.2 Blockchain Network Risks
- **Network Failures**: Blockchain networks may experience downtime
- **Congestion**: High network usage may delay transactions
- **Forks**: Network forks may create ambiguity
- **51% Attacks**: Smaller networks may be vulnerable

### 2.3 Wallet Security Risks
- **Private Key Loss**: Lost private keys mean permanent asset loss
- **Phishing**: Fraudulent websites may steal credentials
- **Malware**: Malicious software may steal keys
- **Social Engineering**: Scammers may trick you into revealing keys

### 3.2 Guardian Network
- **Guardian Unavailability**: Guardians may be unreachable
- **Guardian Disputes**: Guardians may disagree
- **Guardian Compromise**: Guardians may be compromised
- **Guardian Error**: Guardians may make mistakes

### 3.3 Encryption Risks
- **Future Computing**: Quantum computing may break current encryption
- **Implementation Errors**: Encryption may be implemented incorrectly
- **Key Management**: Encryption keys may be lost or stolen
